Bedside EMR · In-room mounting
The challenge

Real-time charting and bedside bar code scanning needed to happen in the room without breaking eye contact or creating tripping hazards. Most rooms had limited wall space and legacy power locations.

The Seamless approach
  • Room-by-room survey of wall, headwall, and footwall mounting options.
  • Standardized articulating arms with low-profile stow, sized to each room type.
  • Scanner brackets and cable paths planned to keep surfaces cleanable.
  • Installs completed in off-peak hours with rooms returned in clinical-ready condition.
Outcome

Clinicians can chart, scan, and interact with patients in a single motion. Cables stay out of the way and infection control teams get fully wipeable surfaces.

Seamless Desktop — Patient-facing areas
The challenge

Registration desks sat in full view of patients. Over time, cables, adapters, and small devices turned into a visible “spider web” that trapped dust and made cleaning difficult.

The Seamless approach
  • Documented every device, cable, and power requirement at each station.
  • Introduced under-surface routing, raceways, and right-sized cable lengths.
  • Repositioned equipment so environmental services could fully wipe surfaces.
  • Standardized layouts so new staff know exactly where equipment lives.
Outcome

Patients now see clean, open work surfaces. Staff can clean in minutes, and IT can service equipment without fighting a knot of cords.

The challenge

OR & anesthesia cart retrofits
The challenge

Anesthesia carts were already packed with critical devices. Teams needed an additional display, keyboard, and scanner without compromising clearances or tipping risk.

The Seamless approach
  • Catalogued all existing equipment and weight distribution on the cart.
  • Added mounting channels matched to cart geometry and OR clearances.
  • Selected mounts tested for load, tilt, and rotation.
  • Built a demo cart, iterated with anesthesia teams, then scaled to all units.
Outcome

Anesthesia teams gained full EMR access at the cart. OR turnover stayed on time, and installs happened outside of scheduled cases.

Surgery tracking boards · Multi-site
The challenge

A visibility initiative called for tracking boards in very different spaces: bright waiting rooms, controlled OR corridors, and staffed nurse stations— all with different viewing distances.

The Seamless approach
  • Matched display size to average viewing distance and line of sight.
  • Specified commercial-grade LEDs with 24/7 runtime for critical boards.
  • Positioned units relative to ambient light and activated auto-dimming where needed.
  • Used low-profile mounts and thin bezels in corridors to meet safety clearances.

L&D fetal monitoring carts
The challenge

Single all-in-one PCs forced clinicians to toggle between charting and fetal strips, creating friction in high-acuity situations. New carts were not in budget.

The Seamless approach
  • Evaluated existing wood carts for structural support and clearances.
  • Designed a vertical dual-monitor bracket that did not increase the footprint.
  • Positioned the strips monitor above and charting monitor at eye level.
  • Completed retrofits in batches to avoid disrupting coverage.
Outcome

L&D staff can now see strips continuously while charting. Carts remain mobile and familiar, but offer a dramatically better workstation experience.

The challenge

As more technology entered the room, keyboards, mice, and cables became difficult to disinfect. Cluttered surfaces made thorough cleaning unrealistic.

The Seamless approach
  • Introduced wipeable, flat-surface keyboards and mice where appropriate.
  • Re-routed and bundled cabling to open up work surfaces.
  • Worked with infection control to align hardware choices with cleaning protocol.
  • Standardized layouts so EVS knows exactly what “clean” should look like.
